## VOICE BRIEFING SCRIPT
### "The HCRU Study" — 90-second prep before a hospital admin or VAC conversation

---

Here is what you now have in your hands.

A paper published March 30, 2026, in the Canadian Urological Association Journal — peer-reviewed, real-world data from the Premier PINC AI™ Healthcare Database. More than 15,000 ureteroscopy patients. Propensity score matched. Multivariable regression. This is not a bench test. This is claims data from US hospitals.

The finding: patients who had their ureteroscopy done with LithoVue Elite — the one with real-time intrarenal pressure monitoring — were significantly less likely to show back up in the emergency department or get admitted to the hospital afterward.

The numbers:

At 10 days: 4.5% of LVE patients had an ED visit or inpatient admission. 8.7% in the other single-use group. That is nearly half the rate. Odds ratio 0.355. p=0.004.

At 30 days: 7.2% versus 12.0%. Odds ratio 0.473. p=0.005.

What does that mean operationally? For every 21 to 24 patients treated with LVE instead of a standard single-use scope, one ED visit or hospital admission is avoided.

In a program doing 500 ureteroscopy cases a year, that is roughly 20 to 25 avoidable return visits — each of which costs a facility in ED staff time, bed time, and CMS quality exposure.

This is not a device comparison on image quality or deflection angle. This is an outcomes study showing that the scope with IRP monitoring is associated with measurably better post-procedure resource utilization.

When you walk in to see a hospital administrator, a CFO, or a value analysis committee, this is your first sentence: "I want to show you a study published last month that quantifies the reduction in ED visits and inpatient admissions when your surgeons use a scope that monitors intrarenal pressure in real time."

Then you stop talking and let them read.

## STUDY INTEGRITY POINTS (use when challenged)

- Database: Premier PINC AI™ — 25% of all US hospital admissions, 1,370+ facilities, 341M+ unique patients
- Design: Propensity score matched 1:3; adjusted for age, sex, race, comorbidity (CCI), payer, diabetes, obesity, prior sepsis
- Population: Real-world, all-payer, nationally distributed — not a controlled trial at a referral center
- Limitation to acknowledge: Retrospective, observational. Cannot confirm whether or how surgeons used IRP data intraoperatively. Early adoption population may include more experienced surgeons.
